E-foil Remote

My objective was to create a waterproof remote E-foil remote that incorporated two control inputs. In this case a Trigger to control the throttle of the vehicle and a thumbwheel to control pitch and roll. The device has to be well balanced and integrate all the required electronics.

The project began by getting the ergonomic shape sorted. The trigger and thumbwheel were moulded into a clay model that was tweaked and shaped until it clearly fit the general persons hand comfortably. The clay was hardened and rechecked

I used a shining 3D scanner was used to scan the object. A point cloud was generated from the scan and Poisson Surface reconstruction calculation was performed to create a watertight mesh. I printed that mesh so that it could be tested for further tweaking.

Now that the ergonomic shape has been found I used that shape combined with the area taken by electronics and battery to flesh out the rough shape of the surface model. Some sketches helped to visualise the shape before going into CAD.

Then I modelled the surface with T-splines, This was quite time intensive I learnt a bunch of tricks to make it faster and as far as I know this is one of the best methods to make a shape that is this organic. Many solid modelling features were then added to finish the remote. Squish percentage for sealing O-ring was calculated and tested. All in all a complicated project requiring a few different tools to complete.

Industrial Robot Cells Development

For Micropsi Industries I developed three robot cells to demonstrate use cases of the product. Micropsi had a novel vision system that used reinforcement learning to move towards a target camera image by associating pixel values with direction vector towards the target.

The Above video is of a customer aerofoil that is has flexible fasters inserted. The difficulty here that the fasteners morph shape over a number of cycles simulating the variability of these parts in manufacture. Through training the range of fastener shapes that are possible the machine learning is able to account for these differences. For this project I organized the props from the customer, designed the table/cell and transportation system, assembled everything, learnt how to program industrial robots, setup the robot designed camera mounts and gripper fingers, programmed the robot, setup safety bounding box. All cell processes ran without errors demonstrating the product well. I decided to contact this customer in particular and use their parts because this demonstration is particularly eye catching. Aerospace parts were interesting to many of our potential customers at tradeshows and these flexible parts demonstrated the advantage of our system well.

Above is another of the three cells I developed. This one demonstrates highly reflective parts that are difficult for conventional vision to deal with.

Archery Bow Grip

I started doing archery back in 2023, got pretty into it. I progressed to shooting out to 60 meters. However when I started training with broadheads my accuracy reduced dramatically. I realized this is due to the fact that I was applying a torque at the handle of the bow. With no broadhead the misalignment of the arrow compared to the string velocity vector is quickly righted by the fletching. However when a broadhead is added there are essentially fletching now also at the front of the arrow, meaning that misalignment creates unstable flight. I designed a extra wide bow grip to push more easily through the thumb muscle avoiding the hand wrapping around the grip and applying torque to the bow. Designed in fusion 360 using a mixture of solid and surface modelling.

From there I was were able to 3d print and tweak the design in CAD based off the point cloud from the scan

Bubble Machine MENG Capstone Project

My MENG masters Capstone team project was the full design and analysis of a marketable Bubble Machine.

Our team has partnered with the toy company Move2Play to design interactive toys that encourage children to play with each other and the world around them. Move2Play is specifically aiming to enter the bubble toy market as it is one of the most popular toy markets on the Amazon storefront. Playing with bubbles has also been linked to children participating in more explorative and creative play, meaning that a bubble toy would perfectly fit Move2Play’s mission of inspiring active play. After analyzing the market, we have identified an untapped niche - large bubbles. Most current bubble toys create bubbles with a few centimeters diameter, and Move2Play believes that blowing significantly larger bubbles will help differentiate their product from others on the storefront.

Link to final paper: Move 2 play capstone Final Paper.docx

When approaching the design of our product the team used an iterative process of design. This process moved through three stages: sketch models, component optimization, and final production. The sketch model phase involved performing teardowns of competing products, rapid prototyping of potential concepts, and selection of a general design framework. Once a design was settled on, the team developed an adjustable prototype that allowed us to optimize the key dimensions and designs of our toy. The team examined the optimal bubble launch angle, fan design, nozzle dimensions, and wand design using both analytical and experimental methods to find specifications that met our design requirements. Finally, once all variables were solidified the team constructed a final prototype that was miniaturized to fit inside of a character chosen to appeal to the young age demographic of the toy.

I designed a custom fan optimized to achieve more efficient flow, therefore reducing noise and power consumption and drastically lowering the overall cost. The goal of the fan design was to create an impeller and housing volute that can easily be injection molded and operated with a cheap DC motor. By using Bernoulli's equation and fan design empirically determined knowledge I was able to design a fan that easily met our requirements.

Our prototype was analyzed using image processing and playtesting to validate our design and confirm that larger bubbles did increase kids’ enjoyment of the toy. When analyzing bubble size we found that our design’s bubbles had an average diameter of 8.7 cm in comparison to the 3.05 cm and 1.81 cm diameters produced by competing toys. Additionally, children remained engaged with our toy for ~25 minutes each play session compared to ~20 minutes with the competing toy. These results validate our design and confirm that we did not only surpass our metrics of success, but the toy is desirable to our target markets.

'Orbuddy' Human Centered Design Project

This project was for a Human Centered Product Design class focusing on the early stages of product development. Super interesting class looking at the fundamentals of design and MVP's proving out product concepts. The class problem that weas given is a widespread concern of the public: excess sedentary behavior. An unhealthy sedentary lifestyle could lead to serious health issues. It is estimated that physical inactivity is the cause of 6% of the burden of disease from coronary heart disease, 7% of type 2 diabetes, and 10% of breast cancer. Inactivity causes 9% of all premature mortality.

Figure: Storyboard to justify product functionality.

Young professionals (age 22-29) need a sustainably habit-building method of keeping their muscles loose. This demographic tends to see a steep decrease in physical activity in a short amount of time, which can have long-term negative impacts as they age. Products and services on the market currently do not address these young working professionals who don’t have a lot of opportunities or time in their schedules to be active, don’t have a pre-existing regular exercise regimen, and need external motivation through social means to introduce healthy lifelong habits for their long term health, physically and mentally, to keep their muscles loose and personal connections strong.

We formed this product opportunity gap (POG) statement after generating our key social, economic, and technological (SET) factors. Socially, it is a trend that during tertiary education people are relatively active compared to when they start working and generally become more sedentary. This is due to a number of complicated factors such as having money to buy cars and not having to move once arriving at the workplace or not travelling to a workplace at all. In the economic sense, companies are investing more in the health of their employees to offset insurance payouts. This leads to a rise in demand for health products. The technology that we have today has huge potential to create very innovative devices in this field.

Figure: Persona map for Overly Sedentary Owen

The final concept, Orbuddy, is a small desktop friend that reminds the user to take active breaks through gentle light notifications. A removable “hat” piece acts as an activity tracker that can be customized to the user’s personal taste. This piece docks on the Orbuddy base, an ovoid light with a friendly face, to record activity and register working status. The user can additionally register friends’ Orbuddy units to show their working statuses as well, providing additional motivation for the user to take breaks with their friends. A proof-of-concept prototype of this final concept was developed using 3D-printed parts and an Arduino nano circuit.
